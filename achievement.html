Our Achievements

28th DSPC

Journalism has struggled to emerge victorious against the rampant proliferation of misinformation within our society. With this, campus journalists are challenged to battle the said predicament within their communities. They strive to instigate progressive change using the power of knowledge and truth through various forms of journalism. Presenting, the 28th Division Schools Press Conference (DSPC) winners in the high school category! May the spirit of campus journalism remain alive and burning in our PCC community by being heralds of truth in diversity.

29th DSPC

Eight PCCians from junior and senior high schools (JHS and SHS) achieved success in the past 29th DSPC. The annual contest of campus journalists was held at Pasig Elementary School on December 2nd, and the award ceremony took place a week later, on the 9th of the same month.

30th DSPC

Students from various schools in Pasig gathered together at the 30th Division Schools Press Conference (DSPC) Awarding Ceremony held at the Pasig Elementary School Gym yesterday, December 2, 2024. This event brought out their skills in writing, broadcasting, and storytelling, highlighting the importance of responsible journalism while inspiring the youth to use their voices to inform, empower, and bring about change.

2023 PhotoJourn RSPC

Our dearest congratulations to Gabriel Calica, who won 2nd Place in the Pagkuha ng Larawang Pampahayagan category, secondary level, as one of the representatives of Pasig Catholic College and the Division of Pasig in the 2023 Regional Schools Press Conference.

9th PCMMA

The SHS Crusader emerged victorious in the 9th Pasig Catholic Mass Media Awards (PCMMA) for winning an award in the Academic Category.
